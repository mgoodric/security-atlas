# Information Security Policy

## Purpose

This policy establishes the framework under which the organization protects the
confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information assets and the
information systems that store, process, or transmit them. It defines the
organization's commitment to security as a board-level concern and binds every
member of the organization — employees, contractors, vendors with access to
the organization's systems — to a baseline set of expectations.

## Scope

This policy applies to:

- All employees, contractors, interns, and temporary staff
- All information assets owned by or in custody of the organization, regardless
  of format (electronic, paper, voice, video)
- All information systems, including those operated by third parties on the
  organization's behalf
- All locations from which information is accessed, including remote work
  environments and personal devices that touch organizational data

## Policy

The organization:

1. Treats information security as a foundational operating concern, not a
   compliance afterthought. Security commitments are stated by leadership,
   resourced annually, and reviewed at least quarterly by the security lead
   and at least annually by the board or executive equivalent.
2. Maintains a security program built on a documented framework
   (the Secure Controls Framework, with crosswalks to SOC 2, ISO 27001, and
   NIST CSF). The program is auditable end-to-end through this platform.
3. Classifies information by sensitivity (`restricted`, `confidential`,
   `internal`, `public`) and applies handling rules proportional to the
   classification. The data handling matrix is published separately and
   reviewed annually.
4. Operates a risk register. Risks are reviewed quarterly; treatment is
   documented; residual risk is approved by the accountable role for that
   risk's level (team, organization, or company).
5. Reviews every supporting policy on a recurring cadence — at minimum
   annually, or when material change occurs in the technology stack, the
   regulatory environment, or the threat landscape.

## Roles and responsibilities

- **Board / executive sponsor** — approves this policy; reviews the security
  program at least annually; accepts residual risk at the company level.
- **Security lead** — owns this policy; maintains the supporting policies;
  reports security posture to leadership monthly; signs off on
  organization-level risk treatment.
- **Engineering, IT, and operations leads** — implement controls in their
  respective domains; report material control failures within one business day;
  own team-level risk treatment.
- **All personnel** — read, acknowledge, and follow this policy and the
  supporting policies linked from it.

## Enforcement

Violations may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination
of employment or contract. Violations that suggest a control failure are
treated as security incidents and follow the Incident Response Plan.

## Review and revision

This policy is reviewed annually by the security lead and re-approved by the
executive sponsor. Material changes between annual reviews require approver
sign-off and trigger a re-acknowledgment campaign.
