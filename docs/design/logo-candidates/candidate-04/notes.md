# Candidate 04 — Node-graph A (8-color positional gradient, densified)

**Concept:** A node-and-edge graph (16 lines, 14 visible nodes) whose silhouette implies a stylized capital A. Filled dots at every structurally significant intersection make the "graph" reading explicit — directly evoking the control-graph data model from canvas §3 (one control, N framework satisfactions via STRM-typed edges through SCF anchors). v6 densifies v5's geometry (11 → 16 lines, 12 → 14 nodes) per maintainer ask ("more lines, similar to how the logo was originally, not quite as many, but more"), and replaces v5's 4-color pastel/sky palette with the maintainer's new 8-color wide-spectrum temperature gradient applied positionally — warm pinks at the apex, cream/mint at mid-A, sky/blue at the foundation. Stroke weight is uniform (sw=6) across all 16 lines; color carries the visual hierarchy.

**Color treatment:**

With 16 lines / 8 colors / 3 weight tiers all competing, the mark reads as visual confetti; uniform weight lets color carry the structure cleanly. Sister L / R lines share a color so the gradient reads as left-right-symmetric rather than scattered.
- The 8 maintainer-supplied pastels are `#f2a2b3`, `#f9c3c3`, `#f7d4c0`, `#f9e6c1`, `#d1e7e0`, `#a0d1e8`, `#7ab8e1`, `#4b8db5`. **Strengths:** v6 honors both maintainer asks faithfully — density (16 lines, between v5's 11 and v1's ~15) and palette breadth (the full 8-color warm→cool spectrum). The positional gradient reads coherently: warm pinks at the apex give the mark visual lift, cream / mint at mid-A sit as a tonal pivot, and the sky→deep-blue lower zone anchors the mark in a foundation tone. Sister L/R lines sharing a color preserves the strong vertical symmetry of the A silhouette — the gradient does NOT read as random or confetti. Uniform stroke weight (sw=6) gives all 16 lines equal structural weight, so the mark reads as "a single graph with 16 edges colored along a spectrum" rather than "edges in 3 weight tiers with arbitrary colors". The 8-color spread is dramatically wider than v5's 4-pastel palette — the mark now spans the entire warm-cool axis, which the maintainer's ask explicitly requested. Topology discipline is preserved (32/32 endpoints matched, the v4 invariant continues to hold for v6's denser geometry). The dark variant carries the maintainer's pastels verbatim and reads as airy + warm-anchored at the top. The light variant's Tailwind-derived 700-800 weight complements produce a serious, editorial reading consistent with a security/GRC tool — and notably v6 clears not just SC 1.4.11 but also SC 1.4.3 (4.5:1) on ALL 16 slots, a strict improvement over v5. Files remain well under budget (combined 173.2 KB / 600 KB).

**Weaknesses:** The 8-color positional gradient surrenders v5's three-tier weight hierarchy. A viewer accustomed to "thick line = important, thin line = decorative" loses that depth cue in v6 — there is now only color to differentiate roles, not weight. For viewers who read graph diagrams as "topology + weight" this is a regression. The split-palette approach (pastels on dark, complements on light) remains in place from v5, but v6 ALSO drops v5's shared `#517891` brand-through-line because no color in the v6 palette clears SC 1.4.11 on both backgrounds. The two variants now share zero colors. The dot color (`#1e40af` on light / `#4b8db5` on dark) sits in the same color FAMILY across variants but is not pixel-identical; the family-resemblance signal is weaker than v5's literal shared color. At favicon scale (16 px) the 5 new lines (apex roof, upper crossbar, inner diagonals) will visually merge with their neighbors and the gradient will collapse to a single dominant mid-tone — the favicon will read as a denser-than-v5 silhouette but the color spectrum will not be discernible at that size. The warm half of the palette (pinks / peach / cream) is a notable brand-personality departure from any prior iteration — v6's first impression is friendlier / softer than v3-v5's serious indigo or v5's professional pastels. For a security platform that may be a deliberate counter-positioning move (security tools are usually grim) or a brand mismatch (security buyers may not respond to pink). The orchestrator and maintainer will need to evaluate this at the candidate-selection layer.

The complement set: `#9f1239` rose-800 ↔ `#f2a2b3` pink; `#be185d` pink-700 ↔ `#f9c3c3` pale pink; `#9a3412` orange-800 ↔ `#f7d4c0` peach; `#854d0e` amber-700 ↔ `#f9e6c1` cream; `#065f46` emerald-800 ↔ `#d1e7e0` mint; `#075985` sky-800 ↔ `#a0d1e8` pale sky; `#0369a1` sky-700 ↔ `#7ab8e1` medium sky; `#1e40af` blue-800 ↔ `#4b8db5` deep blue. All 8 complements clear SC 1.4.11 on light bg with ratios spanning 5.68:1 to 8.36:1.
4. **Color application shifted from weight-keyed to position-keyed.** v3 / v4 / v5 keyed color to stroke-width (each weight tier got its own color). v6 abandons the weight-keyed pattern: 16 lines with uniform stroke (sw=6) and 8 colors arranged along a warm→cool gradient based on each line's midpoint y-coordinate. Upper zone (y_mid < 390): 5 lines using rose-800 / pink-700 / orange-800. Middle zone (390-620): 6 lines using amber-700 / emerald-800 / sky-800. Lower zone (y_mid > 620): 5 lines using sky-700 / blue-800. Sister L / R lines share a color so the gradient reads as left-right-symmetric rather than scattered. This is a structural shift — the tool name `recolor_by_weight.py` is now historical; v6 recolors by position, not by weight. The docstring documents the shift.
5. **Stroke weight uniform (sw=6) across all 16 lines.** v5 used 3 weight tiers (14 / 8 / 4 px). v6's decision: with 16 lines × 8 colors, adding a 3-tier weight hierarchy on top produces visual confetti — the eye cannot read three orthogonal axes (position, color, weight) simultaneously on a 1024px mark. v6 chose uniform weight so that color carries the entire visual hierarchy. The sw=6 value sits between v5's MEDIUM (8) and LIGHT (4); on a denser graph this produces a more "wireframe" reading than v5's "primary backbone + scaffolding" reading.
6. **Node dots uniform deepest tone per variant — drops v5's shared brand-through-line.** v5 used `#517891` for nodes on BOTH variants because it was the one user-supplied pastel that cleared SC 1.4.11 on both backgrounds. v6's new 8-pastel palette has no color with that property — even `#4b8db5` (the deepest pastel) sits at 3.48:1 on light bg, which clears SC 1.4.11 but is too low to use as a dominant node color on light. v6 therefore uses `#1e40af` (blue-800) for nodes on the light variant and `#4b8db5` (deep blue pastel) for nodes on the dark variant — different colors, same FAMILY (deep blue), same role (the deepest tone, anchoring all 14 dots). The two variants no longer share any pixel-identical color; the family-resemblance signal is weaker than v5's shared color, but the warm→cool gradient itself is recognizably the same composition on both renders, so the marks still read as "two variants of the same mark" rather than "two unrelated marks".
7. **`tools/logo-gen/recolor_by_weight.py` extended.** The active `LIGHT_TO_DARK` dict now holds the v6 8-entry mapping (all 8 entries are distinct on both sides — v5's no-op `#517891 → #517891` pair is gone since v6 has no color that's preserved across variants). Tradeoff surfaced: v6 has TWO weakened symmetries vs v5. (a) The weight-tier hierarchy is gone — uniform sw=6 across all 16 lines means viewers who read "thick line = important" lose that depth cue, with only color remaining to differentiate roles. (b) The shared-color brand-through-line is gone — v5 used `#517891` for nodes on both variants; v6 has no color that survives both backgrounds at the required contrast, so the dot color is variant-specific (blue-800 on light, deep-blue pastel on dark). Both tradeoffs are direct consequences of accepting the maintainer's wide-spectrum palette ask faithfully — alternatives (e.g., shoehorn a 3-weight hierarchy into 8 colors, or pick one color from the palette and use it for nodes on both variants regardless of contrast) would all weaken the gradient reading or compromise accessibility. The maintainer's framing ("more wide ranging color palette") makes the gradient the load-bearing visual element; v6 prioritizes it.

Prompt-engineering insight worth recording: when a brief asks for a "wider color palette" with a specific 8-color spec, the right structural response is to make color the dominant compositional axis and drop competing axes (weight tiers, shared-color brand devices) that would muddy the spectrum reading. v6's drop of v3-v5's weight-keyed pattern is a deliberate decision in that direction. Recording the rationale here so future iterations don't re-introduce weight tiers without first reconciling them against the gradient.

Giving named paths converts a vague accessibility concern into a one-question decision. The maintainer's chosen path — "split palette per variant" — is unusual but legitimate, and it surfaces a useful design question (do the two variants need to be photographic inversions of each other, or only family-resemblances) that the v3/v4 iterations had not posed.

**v4 (2026-05-15)** — replaced v3 in response to maintainer feedback on PR #180 ("the lines are no longer connecting, and I was hoping for more contrasting colors"):

1. **Topology rebuilt on a strict node-set** — v3's geometry had line endpoints that didn't match any circle (e.g., the outrigger braces ran from `(320, 250)` and `(704, 250)` — neither of which is a node — out to the outriggers, so the mark read as "line floats, dot floats beside it"). v4 defines a 12-node coordinate table in the SVG header comment, and every one of the 11 `<line>` elements starts and ends at exactly one of those 12 coordinates. The post-render topology check (`python3` scan of all `<line>` endpoints vs all `<circle>` centers) confirms 22/22 endpoint-node matches within 0.5px, 0 broken. Geometry redesign details: the v3 outrigger braces (which ran to non-node coordinates) were re-anchored to the `LEFT_APEX_DETAIL` / `RIGHT_APEX_DETAIL` nodes (the existing apex-tangent dots) — that single change re-uses two existing nodes for three lines each instead of inventing floating endpoints. The remaining 8 lines (2 legs + crossbar + 5 medium connectors) were already terminating at nodes in v3 and were kept as-is.
2. **Wider color spread within the indigo family** — v3's three tiers (indigo-900 / indigo-700 / indigo-600 on light bg; indigo-200 / indigo-300 / indigo-400 on dark bg) clustered closely on the value scale and read as a single tonal band. v4 spreads the tiers further apart: light bg uses indigo-950 / indigo-600 / indigo-500 (15.32:1 / 6.02:1 / 4.28:1); dark bg uses indigo-100 / indigo-300 / indigo-400 (16.07:1 / 9.93:1 / 6.64:1). The HEAVY backbone is now visibly the darkest element; MEDIUM connectors sit in the recognizable indigo brand-primary mid-range; LIGHT scaffolding sits one shade lighter still. No non-indigo accent introduced — brand discipline preserved.
3. **WCAG standard correction** — slice 074 AC-4 cited WCAG 2.2 ≥4.5:1, but that is the text-contrast floor (SC 1.4.3). The correct floor for logo marks (which are graphical objects required to understand the content) is SC 1.4.11 Non-text Contrast at ≥3:1. v4 adopts the SC 1.4.11 floor, which made the wider spread possible (the lightest tier, `#6366f1` on light bg at 4.28:1, would have failed AC-4's literal 4.5:1 read but cleanly passes the standard that actually applies). All six v4 tier colors pass SC 1.4.11; HEAVY + MEDIUM also clear 4.5:1 on both bgs.
4. **Color-mapping table updated in `tools/logo-gen/recolor_by_weight.py`** — the `LIGHT_TO_DARK` dict reflects the v4 indigo-950/600/500 → indigo-100/300/400 swap. The v3 mapping is retained as a commented-out `LIGHT_TO_DARK_V3` block for traceability.
5. **Topology check is now a documented gate for future revisions** — recorded in the SVG header comment + this notes file + the script docstring. Any future revision MUST re-run the `<line>` endpoint vs `<circle>` center check and post a 22/22 (or equivalent for new line counts) before commit.

Prompt-engineering insight worth recording: when a hand-authored SVG mark uses a graph metaphor where "every line terminates at a node" is part of the concept, encode that as a coordinate-table discipline (define the node-set first, then author lines using ONLY those coordinates) and as a post-render verification check — not just as an author-time intention. v3 was authored with the intention but without the check; the topology drift was easy to miss visually at preview size but obvious at zoom. The check is ~15 lines of stdlib Python and runs in milliseconds.

**v3 (2026-05-15)** — replaced v2 in response to maintainer feedback on PR #180 ("more than 2 different line thicknesses with different colors for each weight of line that are aligned with the color pallet of the app"):

1. **Three distinct line thicknesses** — primary backbone at `stroke-width: 14`, secondary connectors at `stroke-width: 8`, detail scaffolding at `stroke-width: 4`. Plus a 4th visual tier from the filled node circles (radii 20 / 18 / 14 / 10 / 6 px depending on structural role).
2. **A different palette-aligned color per weight tier** — HEAVY indigo-900, MEDIUM indigo-700, LIGHT indigo-600 on light bg; HEAVY indigo-200, MEDIUM indigo-300, LIGHT indigo-400 on dark bg. All six colors are on the application's indigo brand scale (`Plans/mockups/*.html`) and all six clear WCAG 2.2 AA (≥4.5:1) on their target background.
3. **Pipeline switch from Flux to SVG-native** — chose Path C (hand-author SVG, rasterize via cairosvg) over Path A (pure Flux multi-color prompt) and Path B (Flux + PIL recolor by thickness). Rationale: the v2 decisions log D13 already recorded Flux's "indigo attractor renders darker than prompted" finding; with a six-hex spec the failure mode would be magnified. Path C gives bit-perfect hex fidelity, deterministic per-line stroke widths, and a vector source that ships alongside the PNGs as `mark.svg`. The candidate is now the only one in the slate where the mark exists as a checked-in SVG.
4. **New tool: `tools/logo-gen/recolor_by_weight.py`** — token-swaps the three palette hex values to produce the dark-bg variant from the light-bg SVG, then rasterizes both at 1024 and 512 with PNG optimization. Editing the `LIGHT_TO_DARK` dict is how the candidate's palette is re-tuned in the future.
5. **Geometry preserved from v2** — the topology (apex + two shoulders + two leg-bases + outer outriggers + cross-bar midpoint) was traced from the v2 Flux output so the mark reads recognizably as "the same candidate, refined" rather than a fresh design.

Prompt-engineering insight worth recording: when a brief specifies more than one exact hex value, defer to SVG authoring rather than image-model generation. Flux is non-deterministic on multi-color hex specs and its color attractors will pull rendered output away from the spec; cairosvg gives exact per-pixel hex output for the cost of authoring the geometry by hand. For a sparse mark (this one: 11 lines, 12 circles) the hand-authoring cost is minimal and the determinism is permanent. Recipe is in `tools/logo-gen/recolor_by_weight.py`.
